Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 2025

FitIQ Hub ("we," "us," "our," or "Company") operates the fitiqhub.com website (the "Service"). This page informs you of our policies regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of personal data when you use our Service and the choices you have associated with that data.

1. Information Collection and Use

We collect several different types of information for various purposes to provide and improve our Service to you.

1.1 Types of Data Collected

  • Usage Data: We automatically collect information about your interaction with our Service, including the pages you visit, the time and date of your visits, the time spent on those pages, and other diagnostic data.
  • Calculator Data: When you use our calculators, you may enter personal health information (weight, height, age, etc.). This data is processed locally in your browser and is NOT stored on our servers.
  • Device Information: We collect information about the type of device you use to access our Service, including hardware model, operating system, and browser type.
  • Cookies and Similar Technologies: We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to track activity on our Service and hold certain information.

2. How We Use Your Information

FitIQ Hub uses the collected data for various purposes:

  • To provide, maintain, and improve our Service
  • To monitor the usage of our Service
  • To detect, prevent, and address technical and security issues
  • To provide customer support
  • To gather analysis and analytics so that we can improve how our Service works
  • To understand and analyze how you use our Service
  • To communicate with you, including for service-related announcements

3. Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics, a web analytics service provided by Google Inc. ("Google"). Google Analytics uses cookies to help us analyze how visitors use the Site. The information generated by the cookie about your use of the Site (including your IP address) will be transmitted to and stored by Google on servers in the United States.

What Google collects:

  • Pages you visit
  • Time spent on each page
  • How you arrived at our site
  • Your approximate location (city-level)
  • Device type (mobile, desktop, tablet)

What Google does NOT collect:

  • Your personal name or email address
  • Your precise location
  • Any data you enter in our calculators (processed locally only)

5. Data Security

The security of your data is important to us, but remember that no method of transmission over the Internet is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.

Our security measures include:

  • HTTPS encryption on all pages
  • No storage of calculator input data
  • Regular security monitoring
  • GitHub's enterprise-grade security for hosting

6. "Do Not Track" Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" feature. Our Service does not respond to Do Not Track signals. However, you can disable cookies in your browser settings to limit tracking.

We have no control over and assume no responsibility for the content, privacy policies, or practices of any third-party sites or services.

8. Children's Privacy

Our Service does not address anyone under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from children under 13. If we become aware that a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, we immediately delete such information from our servers.

9. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Last updated" date at the top of this Privacy Policy.

You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes. Changes to this Privacy Policy are effective when they are posted on this page.

11. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have specific rights regarding your personal information under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA).

  • Right to Know: You can request what personal information we collect.
  • Right to Delete: You can request deletion of personal information (subject to exceptions).
  • Right to Opt-Out: You can opt-out of the sale of personal information (we do not sell data).

To exercise these rights, contact us at the email above with your request.

12. GDPR Compliance (EU Users)

If you are located in the European Union, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) applies. We comply with GDPR by:

  • Only processing data that is necessary for our Service
  • Obtaining consent before using cookies
  • Allowing users to access, correct, or delete their data
  • Not storing sensitive health data on our servers
